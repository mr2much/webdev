John Taylor Gatto's Biography

A tribute page for a remarkable teacher

It's just impossible for education and schooling to be the same thing. -John Taylor Gatto

Biography

  • He was born on December 15 of 1935 in Monongahela, Pennsylvania. Passed away on October 25th of 2018 in New York City, NY.

  • He started working at an early age, the jobs he did include:

    • Sweeper
    • Snow shoveler
    • Lawn mower
    • Kool-aid and comic book salesman
    • Delivery boy for the Pittsburgh Sun-Telegraph and Uniontown Morning Herald
    • He also had several odd jobs, including taxi driver and hotdog vendor, before becoming a teacher.

  • His other achievements include:

    • Running for the New York State Senate in 1985 and 1988
    • Being named New York City Teacher of the Year in 1989, 1990 and 1991, and New York State Teacher of the Year also in 1991. On that same year he quit teaching, claiming that he was no longer willing to hurt children.
    • 1992 he was named Secretary of Education in the Libertarian Party Shadow Cabinet.
    • 1997 he was given the Alexis de Tocqueville Award of Excellence in Advancement of Educational Freedom

Main Thesis

Gatto asserts the following about schooling:

  • It confuses students by presenting an incoherent and easily forgettable ensemble of information
  • It teaches them to accept their class affiliation
  • It makes them indifferent
  • It makes them emotionally and intellectually dependent
  • It teaches them a kind of self-confidence that requires constant confirmation by experts
